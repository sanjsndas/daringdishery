Cookies Policy

At The Daring Dishery, we use cookies to enhance your culinary journey through our digital hearth. This policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your preferences.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit our website. Like the subtle flavors in a complex dish, they work in the background to enhance your experience, remember your preferences, and help us understand how visitors interact with our content.

These digital morsels are not culinary in nature, but they do help us serve you better by remembering your tastes and preferences as you explore our recipes and stories.

How We Use Cookies

At The Daring Dishery, we use cookies for several purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the website to function properly, like remembering items in your shopping cart or ensuring secure login.
  • Performance Cookies: These help us understand how visitors interact with our website, which pages are most popular, and if users encounter any errors.
  • Functionality Cookies: These remember your preferences, such as your preferred language, region, or dietary restrictions you've indicated.
  • Targeting/Advertising Cookies: These are used to deliver ads relevant to your interests, both on and off our website.

Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Session Cookies To keep you logged in during your visit and maintain your preferences throughout your session Session
Authentication Cookies To identify you when you log into your account and ensure your data remains secure Persistent (up to 30 days)
Preference Cookies To remember your culinary preferences, dietary restrictions, and display settings Persistent (up to 1 year)
Analytics Cookies To help us understand how visitors use our site and improve our content and user experience Persistent (up to 2 years)
Marketing Cookies To deliver personalized culinary content and recommendations based on your interests Persistent (up to 1 year)

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of the website, deliver advertisements on and through the website, and so on. These may include:

  • Google Analytics for understanding how our website is used
  • Social media platforms for sharing content
  • Advertising networks to deliver relevant culinary content

These third-party services have their own privacy policies and cookie policies, which we encourage you to review.

Managing Cookies in Your Browser

You can also manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse to accept cookies and to delete cookies. The methods for doing so vary from browser to browser, and from version to version.

Please note that blocking all cookies may have a negative impact upon the usability of many websites, including ours, as some features may not work properly without cookies.

For more information about how to manage cookies in your specific browser, you can consult your browser's help documentation.
